9-Week Phased Strength Program Advanced

A 9-week phased program that respects fatigue, motor learning, and strength carryover to SBD (Squat, Bench, Deadlift).

Program Overview

This 9-week program is designed to build strength through phased progression: Hypertrophy, Strength, and Power phases, focusing on SBD lifts with accessory work.

  • Weekly Structure: Mon–Tue–Wed–Fri with Thursday rest
  • Phases: Hypertrophy (Weeks 1–3), Strength (Weeks 4–6), Power (Weeks 7–9)
  • Focus: SBD (Squat, Bench, Deadlift) with accessories
  • Progression: Add 2.5–5 lbs weekly if RPE targets are met
  • Autoregulation: Adjust based on fatigue and recovery

Frequency: 4 days/week. Duration: 60-90 min/session.

Phase 1: Hypertrophy (Weeks 1–3)

Increase muscle cross-sectional area, improve work capacity, reinforce technique. RPE: 6–8, Rest: 60–120s, Tempo: Controlled eccentric (2–3s).

Monday – Upper (Chest / Back / Shoulders / Arms)
  • Barbell Bench Press: 4×8 @ 65–70%
  • Barbell Rows: 4×10
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3×10–12
  • Single-Arm Bent Over Rows: 3×12 / side
  • Neutral Grip Shoulder Press: 3×10
  • Lat Pulldowns: 3×12
  • Lateral Raises: 3×15
  • Superset: Bicep Curls 3×12 + Tricep Extensions 3×12
Tuesday – Squat / Quads / Minor Glutes
  • Barbell Back Squat: 5×8 @ 65–70%
  • Hack Squat: 3×10
  • Leg Extensions: 3×12–15
  • Single-Leg Variant (Bulgarian Split Squat): 3×10 / leg
  • Standing Calf Raises: 4×15
Wednesday – Deadlift / Posterior Chain
  • Deadlift: 4×6 @ 65–70%
  • Romanian Deadlift: 3×10
  • Zercher Jefferson Curls: 3×8 (light, slow)
  • Hip Thrusts: 4×10
Friday – SBD + Full Body Accessories
  • Squat: 3×8 @ 65%
  • Bench: 3×8 @ 65%
  • Deadlift: 3×6 @ 65%
  • Accessories (pick 3): Lat Pulldowns 3×12, Lateral Raises 3×15, Hammer Curls 3×12, Dips 3×AMRAP

Phase 2: Strength (Weeks 4–6)

Increase neural efficiency and force production. RPE: 7–9, Rest: 2–4 min, Tempo: Normal to explosive concentric.

Monday – Upper Strength
  • Barbell Bench Press: 5×5 @ 75–80%
  • Barbell Rows: 5×6
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3×8
  • Neutral Grip Shoulder Press: 3×6
  • Lat Pulldowns: 3×8
  • Superset: Hammer Curls 3×10 + Dips 3×8–10
Tuesday – Squat Strength
  • Back Squat: 5×5 @ 75–80%
  • Hack Squat: 3×6–8
  • Leg Extensions: 3×10
  • Bulgarian Split Squat: 3×8 / leg
  • Calf Raises: 4×12
Wednesday – Deadlift Strength
  • Deadlift: 5×4 @ 75–82%
  • RDL: 3×6–8
  • Hip Thrusts: 4×8
  • Zercher Jefferson Curls: 2×6 (very controlled)
Friday – SBD Heavy Technique
  • Squat: 4×4 @ 77–82%
  • Bench: 4×4 @ 77–82%
  • Deadlift: 3×3 @ 80–85%
  • Accessories (2–3 only): Rows or Pulldowns, Lateral Raises, Tricep Extensions

Phase 3: Power (Weeks 7–9)

Maximize rate of force development and peaking readiness. RPE: 6–8, Rest: 3–5 min, Intent: Maximal speed.

Monday – Upper Power
  • Speed Bench Press: 8×3 @ 50–60% (bands optional)
  • Barbell Rows: 4×6
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3×6
  • Neutral Grip Shoulder Press: 3×5
  • Lat Pulldowns: 3×6
  • Lateral Raises: 3×12
  • Superset: Bicep Curls 3×10 + Tricep Extensions 3×10
Tuesday – Squat Power
  • Speed Back Squat: 8×2 @ 50–60%
  • Hack Squat: 3×5
  • Leg Extensions: 3×8
  • Single-Leg Variant: 3×6 / leg
  • Calf Raises: 3×12
Wednesday – Deadlift Power
  • Speed Deadlifts: 6×2 @ 55–65%
  • RDL: 3×5
  • Hip Thrusts: 3×6
  • Optional: Broad Jumps or Kettlebell Swings 4×3–5
Friday – SBD Peak Exposure
  • Squat: 3×2 @ 80–85%
  • Bench: 3×2 @ 80–85%
  • Deadlift: 2×1–2 @ 85–88%
  • Light Accessories Only

Key Suggestions & Autoregulation

  • If Friday fatigue is high: Drop deadlift accessories Wednesday OR reduce Friday deadlift to singles only.
  • Progression model: Add 2.5–5 lbs weekly if all sets hit target RPE.
  • Deload recommendation: 1 week after Week 9 before restarting.

Track your progress weekly. Adjust as needed for recovery. Consult a trainer if unsure.

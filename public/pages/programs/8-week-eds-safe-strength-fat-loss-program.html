8-Week EDS-Safe Strength & Fat Loss Program Intermediate

Designed for females with EDS (Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome) and limited equipment, focusing on joint stability, fat loss, and muscle preservation.

Program Overview

This 8-week program prioritizes joint stability and controlled movements for EDS safety, combining fat loss through density and frequency with muscle preservation via isometrics and unilateral work. Updated to a true 5-day structure for improved fat loss without increasing joint stress.

  • Weekly Structure: Day 1–5 with 2 days off (or active recovery) and optional weekend conditioning
  • Days: Upper Body Stability, Lower Body, Posterior Chain + Core, Mobility + Joint Strength, Full Body Metabolic
  • Equipment: Bodyweight, resistance bands, optional light dumbbell/kettlebell, chair/couch, wall
  • RPE Cap: 6–7, Tempo: 3–1–2, Stop 2–3 reps before fatigue
  • Focus: Joint stability, controlled tempo, no ballistic lifting or end-range locking

Frequency: 5 days/week. Duration: 45-60 min/session.

Weekly Split

5 days on, 2 days off (or active recovery). This structure improves fat loss without adding joint stress.

Day 1 – Upper Body Stability + Shape
  • Incline Push-Ups (wall or bench): 4×8–10
  • Resistance Band Rows: 4×12
  • Seated Neutral-Grip Shoulder Press (band or DB): 3×10
  • Lateral Raises (very light): 3×12–15
  • Bicep Curls (band or DB): 3×12
  • Tricep Kickbacks / Band Extensions: 3×12
  • Wall Angels: 2×10 slow
Day 2 – Lower Body (Quads / Knees / Glutes)
  • Box Squats (chair depth): 4×10
  • Reverse Lunges (short step): 3×8 / leg
  • Glute Bridges: 4×12
  • Low Step-Ups: 3×8 / leg
  • Standing Calf Raises (slow): 3×15
  • Wall Sit: 2×30–45 sec
Day 3 – Posterior Chain + Core
  • Hip Hinges (hands on thighs): 3×12
  • Romanian Deadlift (band/light DB): 3×10
  • Bird Dogs: 3×8 / side
  • Dead Bugs: 3×10
  • Side Plank (knees bent): 2×20–30 sec
Day 4 – Mobility + Joint Strength (NEW)

Active recovery day for nervous system and joint stability.

Block A – Nervous System & Spine (10 min)
  • Diaphragmatic Breathing: 5 breaths
  • Pelvic Tilts: 2×10
  • Cat–Cow (small range): 2×8
Block B – Shoulder & Hip Stability (10–12 min)
  • Band External Rotations: 2×12
  • Scapular Push-Ups (wall): 2×10
  • Standing Hip CARs (controlled): 2×4 / side
  • Glute Bridge Hold: 2×20 sec
Block C – Knees, Ankles, Feet (8–10 min)
  • Terminal Knee Extensions (band): 2×12
  • Short Foot Exercise: 2×8
  • Seated Ankle Circles: 2×10
  • Calf Isometric Hold (mid-range): 2×20 sec
Day 5 – Full Body Metabolic
  • Circuit – 3–4 rounds: Sit-to-Stand Squats ×12, Incline Push-Ups ×10, Band Rows ×12, Marching Glute Bridge ×10, Standing Band Pallof Press ×10
  • Rest 60–90 sec between rounds
  • Optional: Finish with 10–15 min walk

Conditioning & Nutrition

  • Conditioning (2–4x/week): Walking (30–45 min), Cycling, Swimming, Elliptical (NO running, jumping, or HIIT)
  • Nutrition: Small calorie deficit, high protein (1.6–1.8 g/kg), slightly higher sodium intake

EDS-Specific Safety Notes

  • Strength training reduces pain long-term in EDS when controlled
  • Avoid yoga-style deep stretching
  • Hydration + electrolytes matter more than usual
  • Compression sleeves can improve proprioception

This program will burn fat safely, improve muscle tone, reduce joint instability, and build confidence in movement. Consult a trainer if unsure.

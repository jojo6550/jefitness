Full Body Mobility Drills

Improve mobility, posture, and joint function from head to toe.

Program Overview

These drills have been selected to accomplish the following tasks:

  • Improve mobility
  • Improve posture
  • Improve overall joint functions
  • Reduce risk of injury in upper back, lower back, hips, knees and ankles

Recommended Frequency: 3–5 times per week (warm-up, cooldown, or standalone session)

Equipment: Yoga mat or soft surface, chair or wall for balance (optional)

Upper Back – Thoracic Spine

Focus on improving mobility and strength in the upper back, which is crucial for shoulder health and posture.

1. Cat Cow Stretch

Start on all fours. Inhale and arch your back, dropping your belly and lifting your chest and tailbone (Cow). Exhale and round your spine, tucking your chin and pelvis (Cat).

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 | Rest: 1 min

2. Thread the Needle

Start in a tabletop position, i.e., on hands and knees. Slide your right arm under your left arm, reaching as far as possible and lowering your right shoulder and ear to the ground. Breathe deeply and hold the stretch. Return to start, then switch sides.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 (each side), holding 10–20 seconds per rep | Rest: 1 min

3. Thoracic Extensions

Sit on a chair or stand. Place your hands behind your head. Gently arch your upper back and lift your chest without overextending your lower back.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 | Rest: 1 min

4. Wall Angels

Stand with your back against a wall, feet a few inches away. Bend arms to 90° like a goalpost. Slowly raise and lower your arms, keeping elbows, wrists, and back in contact with the wall.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 | Rest: 1 min

Tip: If you can’t keep contact with the wall, start with Floor Angels lying on your back.

Lower Back – Lumbar Spine

Strengthen your core and lower back muscles to enhance stability and prevent discomfort.

1. Dead Bugs

Lie on your back, arms pointing up, knees bent at 90°. Lower your right arm and left leg simultaneously while keeping your lower back pressed into the floor. Return and repeat on the other side.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 per side | Rest: 1 min

2. Bird Dogs

Begin on all fours. Extend your right arm and left leg fully while keeping your back flat. Hold briefly, then return and switch sides.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 per side | Rest: 1 min

3. Glute Bridge

Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Press through your heels to lift your hips, forming a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze glutes, hold briefly, then lower.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 | Rest: 1 min

Shoulder Mobility

Improve range of motion and stability in your shoulder joints, covering flexion, extension, rotation, abduction, and adduction.

1. Shoulder CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)

Stand or kneel. Make a fist and slowly move your arm in a large circle around your body (like a shoulder windmill), rotating the arm internally and externally as needed. Keep your body still and isolate the shoulder.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 | Rest: 1 min

2. Banded Shoulder Dislocates

Hold a resistance band or PVC pipe with a wide grip in front of you. Keeping arms straight, bring it over your head and behind your back, then return. Widen grip if it's too tight.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 slow reps | Rest: 1 min (Do not force reps.)

3. Arm Circles

Stand tall with feet shoulder-width apart. Extend your arms straight out to the sides at shoulder height (T-pose). Begin making small forward circles with your arms, rotating from the shoulder. Gradually increase the size of the circles over 10–15 seconds.

Duration: 30 seconds each side | Rest: 1 min

Ankle – Dorsiflexion

Improve ankle mobility, critical for squats, lunges, and overall lower body performance.

1. Knee Drives

In a half-kneeling position, drive your front knee forward over your toes while keeping the foot flat. Stop when your heel begins to lift. Hold for 3 seconds, then return.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 per side | Rest: 1 min

Note: Do not force your range; go only as far as your ankle allows.

Hip Mobility

Enhance range of motion in your hips, supporting all major leg movements and reducing strain on the lower back.

1. Knee-to-Chest Stretch

Lie on your back. Pull one knee toward your chest while keeping the other leg flat. Hold for 20–30 seconds, switch sides.

Sets: 3 per side | Rest: 1 min

2. Glute Bridge

Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Drive your hips up. Hold at the top for 1–2 seconds, then lower down.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10

3. Seated 90/90 Rotations

Sit on the floor with both legs bent at 90°: front leg in external rotation, back leg in internal rotation. Keep your chest tall. Without using your hands, slowly rotate your knees to switch sides. Move through the range without forcing it.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 8 slow reps each side

4. Standing Hip Abductions

Stand next to a chair or wall for support. Lean your torso slightly forward (about 30–45°), keeping your back flat. Bend the knee of the working (moving) leg. Bring that leg in across your body (toward the midline), squeezing your inner thigh. Slowly return to the start.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10–15 per leg

5. Butterfly Forward Fold

Sit tall with the soles of your feet together and knees dropped to the sides. Hold your feet with your hands. Keeping your spine straight, gently lean forward from your hips (not your lower back) until you feel a stretch in your inner thighs and hips. Hold for 10–20 seconds.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 | Rest: 1 min

6. Rockbacks

Start in tabletop position. Your toes should be pointed outwards. Push your hips back toward your heels, keeping your spine neutral. Move slowly and controlled. Optional: widen knees for a deeper hip stretch.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10

Knee Mobility

Enhance the flexibility and strength around your knee joints for smoother movement and injury prevention.

1. Standing Hamstring Stretch

Place one foot on a low surface. Keep your back straight and bend forward slightly at the hips until you feel a stretch in the hamstrings. Hold for 5 seconds.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 per side | Rest: 1 min

2. Standing Quadriceps Stretch

Stand and hold onto a wall or chair for balance. Grab your ankle behind you and pull gently until you feel a stretch in the front thigh. Keep knees close together. Hold for 3 seconds.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 per side | Rest: 30 seconds

3. Body Weight Squats

Feet shoulder-width apart, chest upright. Lower slowly into a squat, keeping your weight on your midfoot. Drive back up to standing.

Sets: 3 | Reps: 10 | Rest: 1 min

Note: Do ankle and knee mobility drills before this for best results.

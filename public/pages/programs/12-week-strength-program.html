12 Week Strength Programme Advanced

By Jamin Johnson – Build real strength, lose fat slowly, and avoid CNS burnout with phased progression.

Program Overview

This 12-week program is designed to build real strength, promote slow fat loss, and prevent CNS burnout through structured phases and progressive overload.

  • Phased Progression: Hypertrophy (Weeks 1–4) → Strength (Weeks 5–8) → Power (Weeks 9–10) → Deload (Weeks 11–12)
  • 5-Day Split: Focus on compound lifts with accessories for balance
  • Progression: Add 2.5–5 lbs weekly on compounds if all reps are completed with good form
  • Rest: 2–4 min on heavy compounds, 60–90 sec on accessories
  • Conditioning: 2×/week (20–30 min brisk walking, incline treadmill, or light cycling)

Frequency: 5 days/week with 2 rest days. Adjust based on recovery.

Phase 1: Hypertrophy & Foundation (Weeks 1–4)

Focus on building muscle and work capacity with moderate weights and higher reps.

Day 1 – Lower (Squat Focus)
  • Back Squat: 4 sets × 8 reps @ 65–70% 1RM (focus on depth and control)
  • Bulgarian Split Squat: 3 sets × 10 reps/leg (for balance/stability)
  • Leg Press: 3 sets × 12 reps (moderate tempo, full range)
  • Hack Squat: 3 sets × 10 reps (optional if energy allows)
  • Leg Extensions: 3 sets × 12 reps (controlled tempo)
  • Weighted Step-ups: 3 sets × 10 reps (emphasize drive through heel)
Day 2 – Upper (Push Focus)
  • Barbell Bench Press: 4 sets × 8 reps @ 70% 1RM (slow eccentric)
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets × 10 reps (moderate weight)
  • Overhead Press: 3 sets × 8 reps (strict form)
  • Lateral Raises: 3 sets × 12 reps (short rest, muscle burn)
  • Dips: 3 sets × 10 reps (bodyweight or assisted)
  • Triceps Pushdowns: 3 sets × 12 reps (moderate weight)
Day 3 – Upper (Pull Focus)
  • Deadlift: 4 sets × 6 reps @ 70% 1RM
  • Pull-ups / Lat Pulldowns: 4 sets × 8 reps (full stretch)
  • Cable Rows: 3 sets × 10 reps (control and squeeze)
  • Shrugs: 3 sets × 12 reps (top hold 1 sec)
  • Face Pulls: 3 sets × 12 reps (keep elbows high)
  • Dumbbell Curls: 3 sets × 10 reps (strict form)
  • Hammer Curls: 3 sets × 10 reps (slow negative)
Day 4 – Lower (Hinge Focus)
  • Romanian Deadlift: 4 sets × 8 reps (controlled stretch)
  • Hip Thrusts: 4 sets × 10 reps (pause at top)
  • Good Mornings: 3 sets × 10 reps (light-moderate weight)
  • Kettlebell Swings: 3 sets × 12 reps (powerful hip snap)
  • Back Extensions: 3 sets × 12 reps (bodyweight or light plate)
  • Core Circuit: 3 rounds – Planks (45s), Leg Raises (10), Farmer’s Carries (12 steps)
Day 5 – Upper / Weak Points
  • Close-Grip Bench: 3 sets × 8 reps (focus on triceps drive)
  • Lat Pulldowns (wide + narrow): 3 sets × 10 each (barbell curls)
  • Barbell Curls: 3 sets × 10 reps (strict)
  • Rope Pushdowns: 3 sets × 12 reps (control)
  • Dips or Push-ups: 3 sets × AMRAP
  • Farmer’s Carries: 2 sets × 20–30m (grip focus)

Phase 2: Strength (Weeks 5–8)

Lift heavy, reduce volume, maximize neural gains. Cut accessories to 2–3 per session.

Day 1 – Squat
  • Back Squat: 5 sets × 5 reps @ 80–85% 1RM
Day 2 – Push
  • Bench Press: 5 sets × 5 reps @ 80–85% 1RM
Day 3 – Pull
  • Deadlift: 5 sets × 3 reps @ 80–85% 1RM
Day 4 – Hinge
  • RDL / Hip Thrust: 4 sets × 6 reps @ 75–80% 1RM
Day 5 – Weak Points
  • Assistance: 3 sets × 6–10 reps @ 70% 1RM

Long rest (3–4 min) between main lifts. Focus on perfect technique.

Phase 3: Power / Explosive (Weeks 9–10)

Convert strength into speed and power. Keep volume low, focus on bar speed.

Day 1 – Lower
  • Squat (Heavy 2–3 reps) + Jump Squats (6 reps): 4 supersets
Day 2 – Push
  • Bench (3 reps @ 85%) + Plyo Pushups (6): 4 supersets
Day 3 – Pull
  • Deadlift (2 reps @ 90%) + Power Clean (4 reps): 4 supersets
Day 4 – Hinge
  • RDL (3×5) + Kettlebell Swing (3×8)
Day 5 – Upper
  • OHP (3×5) + Medicine Ball Throws (3×8)

Phase 4: Deload / Recovery (Weeks 11–12)

Drop weights to 50–60% of normal. Perform 2–3 sets per lift, moderate tempo. Light cardio, mobility, stretching.

Focus on recovery: Sleep 7–9 hrs nightly, eat well, and prepare for the next cycle.

Nutrition & Recovery

No tracking needed – focus on quality and consistency.

  • Protein: 1g per lb bodyweight (~220g/day). Sources: Eggs, chicken, lean beef, fish, Greek yogurt, whey.
  • Carbs: More on training days, less on rest. Sources: Oats, rice, potatoes, fruits.
  • Fats: From olive oil, nuts, seeds, avocado, salmon.
  • Hydration: At least 3L/day.
  • Sleep: 7–9 hrs nightly.
  • Creatine: 5g/day (optional) for strength and power.

Optional Conditioning

2× per week: 20–30 min incline walk, stationary bike, or sled pushes. Keep heart rate 120–140 bpm (Zone 2).

Track your progress weekly. Adjust as needed for recovery. Consult a trainer if unsure.
